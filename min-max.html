To set continuous rows in cost matrix pricing, set the Min Price for
the next row one (1) cent more than the Max Price of the previous row.

Pricing Definitions/Examples

   No rule defined for a price range uses the suggested retail price.

   Applying a "flat" margin type means the amount added to the list price.
      A flat $10 adjusts a $180 tire to $190.

   Applying a "markup" margin type means the rate multplied by the list price.
      A markup of 10% adjusts a $180 tire to $198.

   Applying a "gross" margin type means the rate at which a "marked down" price equals the list price.
      A gross margin of 10% for a $180 tire means that the adjusted price is $200, i.e. 10% of 200 = $20.
